# NeuroVIT- Pituitary Tumor Detection
As pituitary tumors vary in size, shape, and anatomical position, detecting them with brain MRI is a crucial and difficult task in neuro-oncology. In this work, we introduce Neuro VIT, a hybrid Deep Learning (DL) technique that improves tumor localization and classification by combining U-Net with Vision Transformer (ViT). While the ViT component uses global attention to acquire more comprehensive contextual information, U-Net offers detailed spatial feature extraction for segmentation. The model is evaluated on a pituitary MRI dataset andcompared with State of the Art( SOA) convolutional and transformer-based techniques using standard classification and segmentation metrics. The proposed Neuro ViT performed well across classes, with a classification accuracy of 97%. The model obtained a Precision of 1.00, Recall of 0.95, and F1-score of 0.97 with a support of 1160 samples for the Pituitary class. For the No Tumor class, it achieved a Precision of 0.70, Recall of 0.97, and F1-score of 0.81 based on 140 samples. Furthermore, with a Mean Dice Score of 0.7462, the model showed excellent segmentation performance. The hybrid model continuously showed greater sensitivity to small and subtle tumors and provide more dependable overall performance as compared
to traditional CNN architectures and other cutting-edge deep learning techniques.
